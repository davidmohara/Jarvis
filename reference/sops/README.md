# Standard Operating Procedures

This directory contains SOPs — repeatable processes that have been refined through use.

## Convention

SOPs follow the **2nd/3rd rule**:

1. **First time**: Do the thing. No SOP needed.
2. **Second time**: Notice the pattern. Write the SOP. Document what you did, what worked, what to watch for.
3. **Third time and beyond**: Follow the SOP. Refine it if the process has improved.

SOPs are living documents. Update them when the process changes. Delete them when they're no longer relevant.

## Naming

Files use kebab-case slugs: `one-on-one-prep.md`, `client-meeting-prep.md`, `expense-reports.md`.

## Structure

Each SOP should include:

- **Purpose** — What this process achieves
- **When to use** — Trigger conditions
- **Steps** — Numbered, specific, actionable
- **Notes** — Edge cases, gotchas, tips learned from experience
- **Last updated** — Date of most recent revision

## Relationship to Workflows

SOPs and workflows are different:

- **Workflows** are agent-executable — they have step files, data sources, and the system runs them automatically.
- **SOPs** are human-readable procedures — they document how a recurring process should be done, whether by the system, the controller, or their team.

An SOP may eventually become a workflow if the process is automatable. A workflow may reference an SOP for the human steps it can't automate.
