# YPO Speaker Application — Video Script

**Format:** MP4, 2-3 minutes  
**Tone:** Direct to camera, relaxed, no teleprompter feel  
**Setup:** Seated, camera at eye level, natural light or softbox  

---

My name is David O'Hara. I co-founded a technology consulting company called Improving in 2007. We have grown it to roughly 300 million dollars in revenue, 2,500 people, and 21 offices around the world. I am still here, still building. That matters because everything I am about to say comes from inside that experience, not from studying it.

I sit at an intersection that is genuinely unusual, and I want to describe it honestly rather than pitch it.

The first part is technology. I am a technologist by training and by practice. Improving ships software, AI, and data solutions every day. Right now I am personally leading AI adoption conversations at the executive level through the Dallas Regional Chamber, because I believe the single biggest risk most companies face is not AI itself. It is leadership teams that are waiting to understand it before they engage with it. I have lived the transformation from the inside. That is a different vantage point than most of what you will hear on stage.

The second part is Conscious Capitalism. This is not a philosophy I mention in talks. It is a community I am active in and a framework I have applied building this company. The idea that business is a force for good, that purpose and profit are not in conflict, that culture and stakeholder health are competitive advantages. I believe this because I have tested it. We have built a company where people consistently describe the culture as the reason they stay.

The third part is entrepreneurship. I started from zero in 2007. I was 30 years old. I did not inherit a business. I have experienced what it costs to build something and what it produces when you commit to it fully. I also have active side ventures in medical devices and software because I am genuinely wired to create. I cannot stop.

The fourth part is executive development. I graduated from Stagen Leadership Academy's Integral Leadership Program. I have been an executive coach for the Senior Leadership Initiative since 2013. That is over a decade of sitting across from senior leaders in the most important conversations of their professional lives. I do not study leadership as a discipline. I practice it weekly.

What makes these four things interesting together is that they are not separate tracks. They inform each other constantly. The technology work sharpens my thinking on what conscious leadership actually requires right now. The entrepreneurial experience gives the coaching credibility. The development practice makes me a better builder.

I am a YPO founding member. I know this room. I am not coming in as a speaker for hire. I am coming in as a peer who has something worth the time of the people in it.
