# Gold Forum Retreat - Agenda & Exercises

**Dates:** May 6-8, 2026  
**Location:** Cabo San Lucas  
**Goal:** Create comfort, deepen trust, and strengthen connection — building the foundation for deeper future work.

---

## Pre-Work (Send Today — Before Arrival)

Members should come prepared having done both of the following:

### 1) Pre-Listen: "What If Death Taught Us How to Live?" — David Gibson
[YouTube Link](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=6XsgnDnx03o) | The Crossway Podcast (58 min)

David Gibson, author of *Living Life Backward*, explores how Ecclesiastes brings surprising clarity to daily life. Key themes: the comfort of not being in control, living with uncertainty, and what the end of life reveals about how we should live now.

**Come ready to discuss:** What landed? What challenged you? Where do you feel most out of alignment with what actually matters?

### 2) Pre-Work: Formation Audit (Individual, ~60 min quiet time)
*From Session 02 of Practicing the Way — John Mark Comer*

Work through each section privately before you arrive. Answer honestly and non-judgmentally. Do this slowly, prayerfully, with your heart at peace.

**The five areas:**
- **Habits** — Morning, workday, evening, weekend routines. How are they shaping you?
- **Relationships** — Family, friends, work, community. What does each person draw out in you or suppress?
- **Stories You Believe** — About God, yourself, happiness, the meaning of life. Are they forming or deforming you?
- **Environment** — City, nation, generation, digital algorithm, social/economic group. What's "normal" that may be far from the way of Jesus?
- **Experience** — Family of origin, trauma, key moments, spiritual autobiography. What still needs healing? What must you never forget?

*Note: This is personal. You won't be asked to share everything — but come having done the work.*

## Exercises

### 1) Story Behind the Story
**Time:** 60-90 minutes

Each person shares:
- A story others may already know (success, failure, life event)
- Then: "What did no one see or understand about that at the time?"

**Purpose:** Builds depth without forcing vulnerability. Creates empathy quickly.  
**Tip:** 8-10 minutes per person to keep energy high.

---

### 2) Living Backward Discussion
**Time:** 75-90 minutes  
**Pre-req:** Gibson podcast (pre-listen)

Open group conversation anchored in the podcast. Suggested prompts:

- What in the Gibson episode hit you hardest — and why?
- Where in your life are you living *forward* (chasing, acquiring, performing) when the end of life would tell you it doesn't matter as much as you think?
- What would you do differently if you knew you had 5 years left? What's stopping you from doing that now?
- Where do you feel most out of control — and is that actually a gift?

**Purpose:** Uses Ecclesiastes as a mirror. Surfaces what each person is really oriented around — without making it a therapy session. The podcast does the heavy lifting; you're just responding to it together.  
**Tip:** Let it breathe. Don't rush to resolution. The discomfort is the point.

---

### 3) Formation Audit — Shared Reflection
**Time:** 90-120 minutes  
**Pre-req:** Individual Formation Audit (pre-work)

Each person shares selected highlights from their Formation Audit — not everything, just what surprised them, convicted them, or they want the group to know.

Suggested structure per person (8-12 min):
- One habit or relationship that is forming them well
- One area (story, environment, or experience) where they feel most deformed or stuck
- One thing they want to change — and what's in the way

**Purpose:** Does the work of User Manual of Me *and* Turning Points in one exercise. You learn how each person is wired, what's shaped them, and where they're growing — without it feeling like a biography.  
**Tip:** Confidentiality reminder before starting. "Nothing. Nobody. Never."

---

### 4) Proudest + Hardest (Last 12 Months)
**Time:** 90-120 minutes

Each person shares:
- 1 thing they are most proud of (and why it matters)
- 1 thing that was hardest (feelings, not just facts)

**Purpose:** Balanced reflection. Grounded in the present. Connects naturally to the Formation Audit themes.  
**Tip:** Steer away from purely business updates — this is about the person, not the portfolio.

---

### 5) Appreciations (Closing)
**Time:** 45-60 minutes

Each person shares 1-2 genuine, specific appreciations for others in the group.

**Purpose:** Strengthens bonds. Leaves the group on a high note.  
**Tip:** Be specific — "When you said X, it impacted me because..."

---

### 6) Future Snapshot (Optional — if time allows)
**Time:** 45-60 minutes

Each person: "A year from now, I'd feel great if..."  
Focus on feelings, relationships, and life direction — not goals or metrics.

**Purpose:** Forward-looking alignment after two days of honest reflection. Creates themes for future Forum discussions.

---

## Guardrails
- Don't overpack the schedule — white space is not wasted time
- No alcohol in Forum sessions
- Equal airtime — watch for anyone going long or going quiet
- Recommit to confidentiality before Formation Audit: *"Nothing. Nobody. Never."*
- End sessions while energy is still high — better to leave them wanting more

---

## Core Principle

> The goal is not maximum depth. The goal is building trust, comfort, and connection so deeper work comes naturally in future retreats.

The Formation Audit and Gibson podcast will take people deeper than they expect — which is fine. The guardrails and pacing exist to make sure depth lands as growth, not overwhelm.
