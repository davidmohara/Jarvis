## What You'll Learn

How to close the loop on your day — capture what happened, carry forward what didn't, and set up tomorrow's briefing. This is the bookend to the morning briefing.

## Before We Start

Best done at end of day, after meetings and tasks are complete (or abandoned). If training mid-day, simulate with whatever's happened so far.

## Walkthrough

### Step 1: Trigger the Review (1 min)

Say "daily review" or run `/review-daily`.

**Coaching prompt:** "The daily review takes 5 minutes. It's the easiest 5 minutes you'll spend because it's the difference between tomorrow being proactive or reactive."

Trigger the `chief-review` skill.

### Step 2: What Got Done (3 min)

Chief surfaces completed items — tasks finished, meetings held, delegations closed.

Walk through the list:

**Coaching prompt:** "Take 10 seconds to actually feel good about what you got done. Executives are terrible at this — they jump straight to what's left. The done list matters."

If the done list is thin:
- "Light day for completions. Was it a meetings day, or did priorities shift? Neither is wrong — just name it."

If the done list is full:
- "Strong execution today. Any of these move a rock forward?"

### Step 3: What Didn't Get Done (3 min)

Chief surfaces incomplete items — tasks still open, items deferred from the morning.

For each incomplete item, the user decides:

- **Carry forward** → It stays on tomorrow's briefing with the same priority
- **Reschedule** → Move it to a specific future date
- **Delegate** → Hand it to someone else (creates a delegation)
- **Drop** → It's no longer worth doing

**Coaching prompt:** "If something has carried forward 3+ days, it's either not important enough to do or too important to keep deferring. Pick one."

### Step 4: Capture Loose Threads (2 min)

Ask: "Anything from today that isn't tracked yet? Commitments you made in meetings, follow-ups mentioned in passing, ideas that came up?"

Capture each one. Don't process — just capture. They'll go through inbox processing tomorrow.

**Coaching prompt:** "This is the most important 60 seconds of the review. The things you forget to write down tonight are the things that ambush you next week."

### Step 5: Tomorrow Preview (1 min)

Chief shows a quick preview of tomorrow's calendar and top priorities.

**Coaching prompt:** "Glance at this. Don't plan tomorrow now — that's what the morning briefing is for. Just make sure there are no surprises. If there's a meeting you need to prep for, flag it now and the morning briefing will include a prep reminder."

## Reflection

Ask: "What's the one thing from today that will still matter in a week?"

This builds the habit of distinguishing signal from noise.

## Success Criteria

Run the daily review 3 times. By the third time:
- Review completes in under 5 minutes
- Every incomplete item gets an explicit decision (carry/reschedule/delegate/drop)
- At least one loose thread is captured that wasn't previously tracked
