## What You'll Learn

How to generate crisp talking points for any setting — meetings, panels, podcasts, internal comms, or impromptu conversations — using Harper.

## Before We Start

The user needs an upcoming event where they'll be speaking or presenting a viewpoint. If nothing imminent, ask: "What topic do you most frequently get asked about?"

## Walkthrough

### Step 1: Identify the Context (3 min)

Trigger the `harper-talking-points` skill or say "give me talking points for [event/topic]."

Harper asks:
- What's the setting? (Meeting, panel, podcast, all-hands, 1:1)
- Who's the audience? (Board, clients, team, public)
- What's the objective? (Inform, persuade, align, inspire)
- Time constraint? (30-second elevator pitch or 10-minute presentation?)

### Step 2: Generate Points (5 min)

Harper produces 3-5 talking points, each structured as:

- **Headline** — The point in one sentence
- **Supporting evidence** — One fact, example, or story
- **Pivot line** — How to connect to the next point or field a question

**Coaching prompt:** "Talking points aren't a script. They're anchors. You know these 3-5 things, and everything else flows from them. If you get lost mid-conversation, come back to an anchor."

Walk through each point with the user. Challenge:
- "Is this true?" (Don't put words in their mouth)
- "Is this relevant to this specific audience?"
- "Could you say this in a hallway conversation naturally?"

### Step 3: Stress Test (5 min)

For each talking point, identify the likely pushback:

- "If someone disagrees with this point, what will they say?"
- "What's the hardest question you could get asked about this?"

Draft a response to the toughest question.

**Coaching prompt:** "Preparation isn't about memorizing answers — it's about having thought through the terrain. When the hard question comes, you won't panic because you've already been there mentally."

### Step 4: Delivery Notes (2 min)

Harper adds delivery context based on the setting:

- **Panel**: Lead with your unique angle. Don't repeat what other panelists said.
- **Podcast**: Stories over stats. The listener is driving, not reading a spreadsheet.
- **All-hands**: Start with the headline. End with the ask.
- **Board**: Data first, interpretation second. Don't editorialize.

## Reflection

Ask: "Of these talking points, which one would you lead with? Why?"

The ability to prioritize points shows communication maturity.

## Success Criteria

Generate talking points for 2 events. At least one set should include stress-tested responses to likely pushback.
