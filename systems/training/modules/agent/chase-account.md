## What You'll Learn

How to build a comprehensive account strategy using Chase — mapping the account landscape, identifying whitespace, building a stakeholder map, and creating a multi-quarter growth plan.

## Before We Start

Complete Pipeline Review and Client Meeting Prep. The user should be comfortable with Chase's data capabilities. They need a target account to strategize on — ideally one with existing relationship and expansion potential.

## Walkthrough

### Step 1: Select the Account (3 min)

Trigger the `chase-account` skill or say "build an account strategy for [company]."

Ask: "Which account has the most untapped potential? Where are you leaving revenue on the table?"

### Step 2: Account Landscape (7 min)

Chase assembles:
- Current engagement (active deals, past projects, contract status)
- Revenue history and trajectory
- Key contacts and stakeholder map
- Competitive presence (who else is serving this account?)
- Industry context and trends

**Coaching prompt:** "Account strategy starts with understanding the full picture, not just your slice. What does this company care about? What pressures are they facing? Your strategy should align with their priorities, not just your quota."

### Step 3: Whitespace Analysis (7 min)

Identify expansion opportunities:

- Services you offer that they haven't bought
- Departments you haven't reached
- Problems they have that you can solve but haven't pitched
- Contract renewals or expansion triggers

**Coaching prompt:** "Whitespace isn't just 'things we haven't sold them.' It's problems they have that we're uniquely positioned to solve. Start with their pain, not your catalog."

### Step 4: Stakeholder Strategy (7 min)

Map the relationship landscape:

- **Champions** — Who advocates for you internally?
- **Decision makers** — Who signs the check?
- **Influencers** — Who shapes the decision maker's view?
- **Blockers** — Who would rather you weren't there?
- **Unknown** — Who should you know but don't?

For each: "What's their agenda? What do they care about that has nothing to do with you?"

### Step 5: Playbook (6 min)

Build a 90-day action plan:

1. Immediate actions (this week)
2. Near-term plays (this month)
3. Quarter objectives
4. Relationship investments to make

Save the strategy. Create action items for immediate and near-term plays.

## Reflection

Ask: "If you could only do one thing for this account this quarter, what would move the relationship furthest forward?"

## Success Criteria

Build one account strategy with history, contacts, whitespace, and a 90-day recommended playbook.
