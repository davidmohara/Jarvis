## What You'll Learn

How to prep for client-facing meetings using Chase — attendee research, account history, deal context, and strategic talking points.

## Before We Start

The user needs an upcoming client or prospect meeting. Calendar Prep (chief-calendar-prep) should be familiar since this builds on the same concept but adds revenue-specific depth.

## Walkthrough

### Step 1: Identify the Meeting (2 min)

Pull the calendar and identify a client-facing meeting. If multiple exist, ask: "Which client meeting has the highest stakes this week?"

### Step 2: Trigger Chase Prep (3 min)

Say "prep for my meeting with [client]" or trigger the `chase-client-prep` skill.

Chase pulls:
- Company profile (size, industry, recent news)
- Attendee profiles (titles, LinkedIn context, previous interactions)
- Account history (past deals, current opportunities, interactions)
- Relevant proposals, contracts, or deliverables
- Any open issues or risks

### Step 3: Account Context (5 min)

Walk through the account story:

**Coaching prompt:** "Every client meeting exists in a history. What have we done for them? What are they expecting? What do they think of us? Chase gives you the institutional memory."

Key questions for the user:
- "What do they want from this meeting?"
- "What do you want from this meeting?"
- "Is there misalignment between those two?"

### Step 4: Attendee Strategy (5 min)

For each attendee:
- What's their role in the decision?
- What do they care about? (Technical depth? Business outcomes? Risk?)
- Have you met before? What was the dynamic?

**Coaching prompt:** "Map the room before you walk in. Who's the decision maker? Who's the influencer? Who's the blocker? Tailor your message to each."

For role-specific coaching:
- **CEO**: "Your job isn't to sell in this meeting. It's to establish peer-level trust with their leadership."
- **VP**: "Balance technical credibility with business value. They need both."
- **COS**: "Prepare your exec with the talking points, but also prep for the questions they'll get."

### Step 5: Talking Points and Landmines (5 min)

Chase generates:
- 3-5 talking points aligned to the meeting objective
- Potential objections or difficult questions
- Topics to avoid (recent bad press, sensitive organizational changes)

**Coaching prompt:** "Knowing what NOT to say is as important as knowing what to say. If they just went through layoffs, don't lead with 'growing your team.' Chase flags these."

## Reflection

Ask: "What's the one thing from this prep that would have caught you off guard if you hadn't seen it?"

## Success Criteria

Prep 2 client meetings with account context, attendee research, and talking points. At least one prep should surface a non-obvious insight.
