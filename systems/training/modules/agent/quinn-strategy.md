## What You'll Learn

How to build a real strategy using Quinn's action agenda methodology: diagnosis, guiding policy, coherent actions. Not a wish list. Not a goal statement. A strategy.

## Before We Start

Complete the Rock Review module. The user should have familiarity with their quarterly objectives since strategy often connects to or overrides them.

This is the longest module. It can be split across sessions if needed.

## Walkthrough

### Step 1: Pick the Challenge (5 min)

Trigger the `quinn-strategy` skill or say "help me build a strategy for [challenge]."

Ask: "What's the most important challenge facing your team/organization right now that doesn't have a clear solution?"

**Coaching prompt:** "If the solution is obvious, you don't need a strategy — you need execution. Strategy is for the hard problems where the path forward isn't clear."

### Step 2: Diagnosis (10 min)

Quinn guides the user through understanding the challenge:

- What's actually happening? (Facts, not feelings)
- Why is it happening? (Root causes, not symptoms)
- What's the crux — the critical aspect that, if addressed, would unlock everything else?

**Coaching prompt:** "Most strategies fail because they skip diagnosis. They jump to 'we should do X' without understanding why the current state exists. A doctor who prescribes before diagnosing is dangerous. So is an executive."

Push the user to go deeper on each answer. "That's a symptom. What's causing it?"

### Step 3: Guiding Policy (10 min)

Based on the diagnosis, develop a guiding policy — the approach for dealing with the challenge:

- It's not a goal ("increase revenue" is not a policy)
- It's not a list of actions ("hire 5 engineers" is not a policy)
- It IS a direction and constraints ("focus exclusively on enterprise accounts where we have existing relationships")

**Coaching prompt:** "A guiding policy says what you WILL do AND what you WON'T do. If your policy doesn't exclude anything, it's not a policy — it's a wish."

### Step 4: Coherent Actions (10 min)

Develop 3-5 specific actions that implement the guiding policy:

For each action:
- What exactly will be done?
- Who owns it?
- By when?
- How does it connect to the guiding policy?

Check for coherence: do these actions work together, or do they pull in different directions?

**Coaching prompt:** "Coherent means they reinforce each other. If action 1 is 'focus on enterprise' and action 3 is 'launch a self-serve product for SMBs,' they're incoherent. Strategy is about making choices that compound."

### Step 5: Bad Strategy Check (5 min)

Quinn runs a diagnostic for common bad strategy patterns:

- **Fluff** — Restatement of the obvious in inflated language
- **Failure to face the challenge** — Strategy that doesn't address the actual problem
- **Mistaking goals for strategy** — "Our strategy is to grow 20%" (that's a goal)
- **Bad objectives** — Too many, conflicting, or impractical

**Coaching prompt:** "If your 'strategy' survives by replacing every mention with 'we want to' and still making sense, it's not a strategy — it's a goal list."

### Step 6: Document and Connect (5 min)

Save the strategy with:
- Diagnosis summary
- Guiding policy statement
- Coherent actions with owners and timelines
- Connection to quarterly rocks (which rocks does this affect?)

If actions create new tasks or delegations, capture them.

## Reflection

Ask: "What did the diagnosis reveal that you weren't seeing before?"

## Success Criteria

Build one strategy using the action agenda methodology with all three components (diagnosis, guiding policy, coherent actions). The strategy should pass the bad strategy check.
