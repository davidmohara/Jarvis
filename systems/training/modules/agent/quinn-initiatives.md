## What You'll Learn

How to track strategic initiatives through Quinn — the projects that span multiple rocks, multiple quarters, and multiple people. These are the bigger-than-a-task, smaller-than-a-strategy items that need sustained attention.

## Before We Start

Complete Rock Review and Strategy Builder. The user should have active rocks and at least one cross-cutting initiative.

## Walkthrough

### Step 1: Identify Active Initiatives (3 min)

Trigger the `quinn-initiatives` skill or say "show me my initiatives."

Quinn surfaces current initiatives — projects with defined outcomes, owners, and timelines that connect to one or more rocks.

If none exist, help the user identify one: "What project are you running right now that's bigger than a single task and connects to your quarterly rocks?"

### Step 2: Status Dashboard (5 min)

For each initiative:
- Overall health (green/yellow/red)
- Owner and contributors
- Connected rocks
- Key milestones and their status
- Blockers

**Coaching prompt:** "Initiatives are the middle layer — too big for a daily task list, too small for a full strategy document. They need their own tracking or they fall into the gap between daily execution and quarterly planning."

### Step 3: Dependency Mapping (5 min)

For each initiative, identify:
- What does this depend on? (Other initiatives, external events, decisions)
- What depends on this? (Downstream work that's blocked until this completes)

**Coaching prompt:** "Cross-initiative dependencies are where the most insidious delays hide. Your initiative is green, but it depends on another team's initiative that's red. That makes yours red too — you just don't know it yet."

### Step 4: Blocker Resolution (5 min)

For any blocked or at-risk initiative:
- Name the specific blocker
- Identify who can remove it
- Decide: escalate, work around, or wait?

Capture resolution actions as tasks or delegations.

### Step 5: Update and Communicate (2 min)

Update initiative status. If stakeholders need to be informed, draft a status update.

**Coaching prompt:** "The best initiative managers over-communicate status. If it's green, say it's green and why. If it's yellow, say it's yellow and what you're doing about it. Silence breeds anxiety."

## Reflection

Ask: "Which initiative would hurt most if it failed? Is it getting proportional attention?"

## Success Criteria

Track 2 initiatives through status updates, surface blockers, and connect them to rocks.
