## What You'll Learn

What the CRM connector enables for Chase and the broader system — pipeline data, account records, contact intelligence, and how it transforms revenue management.

## Before We Start

CRM connector must be active in the user's config.

## Walkthrough

### Step 1: What CRM Provides (3 min)

Walk through the data layer:

- **Pipeline** — Deals/opportunities with stages, values, close dates
- **Accounts** — Company records with history, size, industry
- **Contacts** — People with roles, titles, communication history
- **Activities** — Logged calls, emails, meetings, tasks

### Step 2: How Chase Uses It (5 min)

**Pipeline Review**: Live deal data instead of manual entry. Chase reads stages, values, and activity to assess health.

**Client Meeting Prep**: Account history and contact details pulled automatically. No more "let me look that up."

**Account Strategy**: Full picture — past revenue, active deals, key contacts, competitive presence — all from CRM.

**Win/Loss Analysis**: Historical deal outcomes feed pattern recognition. What worked, what didn't, and why.

**Coaching prompt:** "Without CRM, Chase works from what you tell it. With CRM, Chase works from what the whole organization knows. That's the difference between a personal assistant and a strategic advisor."

### Step 3: Beyond Chase (2 min)

CRM data also enriches:

- **Chief**: Surfaces pipeline changes in morning briefings
- **Shep**: Team member account assignments for 1:1 context
- **Quinn**: Revenue-related rock progress tracking

## Reflection

Ask: "What's one question about your pipeline that you can only answer today by digging through the CRM manually?"

That's the first thing Chase should automate for them.

## Success Criteria

Understand what CRM connection enables for Chase. The user can articulate how live pipeline data changes their workflow.
