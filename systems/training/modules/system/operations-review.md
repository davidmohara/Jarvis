## What You'll Learn

The review cadences — daily, weekly, monthly, quarterly — and what each one covers. Reviews are how you zoom out from the daily grind and make sure the machine is working.

## Before We Start

Complete Core Operations. The daily review module is helpful but not required.

## Walkthrough

### Step 1: The Four Cadences (5 min)

Overview of each review level:

**Daily** (`/review-daily`) — 5 minutes. What got done, what didn't, what carries forward. Already covered in the Daily Review module.

**Weekly** (`/review-weekly`) — 15 minutes. Step back from tasks to priorities. Are this week's actions moving the rocks? What's next week's focus?

**Monthly** (`/review-monthly`) — 30 minutes. Pattern review. What's working in the system, what's not? Delegation health, goal progress, team health trends.

**Quarterly** (`/review-quarterly`) — 60 minutes. Rock assessment, strategy check, system evolution. This is where you decide what changes for next quarter.

**Coaching prompt:** "Daily and weekly keep you on track. Monthly and quarterly keep you honest. Most executives do the daily work fine but skip the monthly and quarterly reviews — which is how drift happens invisibly."

### Step 2: Run a Weekly Review (5 min)

Trigger `/review-weekly`. Walk through:

- This week's completions vs. plan
- Rock progress since last week
- Calendar assessment (was time spent well?)
- Next week's priorities

**Coaching prompt:** "The weekly review takes 15 minutes but saves you hours of wrong-direction work. It's the reset point."

### Step 3: Preview the Monthly and Quarterly (5 min)

Don't run a full monthly or quarterly now — just show what they look like:

- Monthly: system health metrics, delegation patterns, team engagement trends
- Quarterly: rock final assessment, next quarter rock setting, system improvements

**Coaching prompt:** "We'll run these for real when the time comes. For now, know they exist. The quarterly review is where evolutions happen — new capabilities get added to IES based on what you've learned."

## Reflection

Ask: "Which cadence do you think you'll skip most? Daily, weekly, monthly, or quarterly?"

Whatever they answer, that's the one Shep should monitor and nudge on.

## Success Criteria

Run daily, weekly, monthly, and quarterly review operations. Mastered when the user has run daily + weekly independently twice each.
