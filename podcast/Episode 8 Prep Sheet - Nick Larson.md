# Episode 8 Prep Sheet
## AI-Ready Data: The New Benchmark of Enterprise Competitiveness
**Guest:** Nick Larson, VP of Technology, Improving
**Date:** TBD
**Prepared:** May 4, 2026

---

## Guest Context

Nick has been at Improving since 2020, when Object Partners merged in. Before that he spent 12 years at Object Partners as VP of Solution Delivery. His background is deeply technical (senior developer roots at Union Pacific, West Corp, MSI) with a long arc toward enterprise solution leadership. He knows the gap between data-in-theory and data-in-practice better than almost anyone because he's lived it across complex engineering environments. Don't treat him as a talking head — he'll have opinions and he'll push back if a question is too surface-level.

---

## Episode Theme

The competitive moat is shifting from who has the best AI to who has the best data foundation underneath it. Most enterprises are overestimating their data readiness, underestimating what agentic AI actually requires, and patching legacy systems with AI layers that are going to crack. This episode names that problem and gives leaders a framework for thinking about it.

## Opening

Warm welcome, brief setup. Suggested framing:

"We talk a lot on this show about what AI can do. Today we're going upstream — to the data that makes it actually work. Nick Larson is VP of Technology at Improving, and he's seen what happens when enterprises try to run before they can walk on data. Nick, welcome."

## Questions and Talking Points

**1. Define the term.**
AI-ready data is almost a buzzword at this point. Take it beyond the buzzword — what does it actually mean?

**2. Data vs. models.**
What's the distinction between data and models, and why has data readiness become the actual competitive advantage rather than the model you pick?

**3. The self-assessment problem.**
Do most enterprises think they're further along on data readiness than they actually are? What's the typical gap between perception and reality?

**4. Garbage in, garbage out — still true.**
AI gets positioned as a miracle worker, but the quality of outputs is entirely bounded by the quality of inputs. Talk about that ceiling and what it means practically for leaders who are buying AI tools right now.

**5. From reporting to decisioning.**
Why does it matter to shift from using data for reporting to using data for real-time decisioning and automation? Is that shift actually happening in the enterprises you work with?

**6. Agentic AI raises the stakes.**
Give the audience a quick grounding in agentic AI, and then explain how it changes the requirements. Systems aren't just analyzing data anymore — they're acting on it.

**7. The widening gap.**
Talk about the competitive divergence between data-mature enterprises and data-chaotic ones. What's the long-term picture for the ones on the wrong side of that line?

**8. AI layered on legacy data.**
A lot of organizations are stacking AI solutions on top of legacy data infrastructure. What actually happens when you do that? And what's the risk of treating it as a permanent fix rather than a bridge?

**9. What good data actually looks like.**
Walk through the markers of AI-ready data:
- Strong metadata, lineage, and observability
- Clear ownership
- Embedded governance
- The components people most commonly overlook
- How do you know when you're actually ready?

**10. Autonomous systems and wrong calls.**
How does data readiness — or lack of it — directly affect the risk of an autonomous system making a bad decision? What's the failure mode?

**11. Crystal ball.**
Two to three years out: what separates companies that experimented with AI from companies that became AI-driven? What's the deciding factor?

**12. Key takeaways.**
What do you want leaders to walk away thinking about today?
