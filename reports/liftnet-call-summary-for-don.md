## The Problem

### Capability Gap
The Guatemala team has no senior engineers. No one coaching on testing, unhappy path handling, code annotation, code reviews — nothing. Stephen describes tasks estimated at two hours that remain unfinished three weeks later. He characterizes the team as "unteachable" after months of effort.

### Process Failure
The team was not documenting anything — no release notes, no meaningful standups, no sprint discipline. The project manager wasn't tracking completions or flagging blockers. Stephen is personally attending every standup and coaching the team daily, but results haven't improved.

### Leadership Disconnect
When Stephen requested a senior engineer to independently assess the team, Rene sent Rodrigo. Rodrigo observed and came back blaming requirements and process — completely ignoring the capability concerns Stephen had specifically asked him to evaluate. Stephen sees this as the Guatemala leadership protecting their team rather than honestly assessing performance.

### Security Exposure
A pen test done 1-2 years ago found 11 vulnerabilities. Stephen re-scanned using AI tooling and found **10 of the 11 still exist** despite the Guatemala team having the remediation list for two years. They're on a SOC 2 certification path and this is a blocker.

### Cost Argument Is Moot
Stephen made this point explicitly: *"The cost differential is mute because I'm spending three times the time fixing things as I would getting outcomes."* The nearshore cost savings are being eaten by his own time investment. He's ready to insource if we can't offer something better.
