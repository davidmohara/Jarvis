# Step 05: People Check

## MANDATORY EXECUTION RULES

1. You MUST review 1:1s that happened this week and 1:1s scheduled for next week.
2. You MUST surface any people issues found in daily reviews, meeting notes, or delegation data.
3. You MUST ask: "Anyone on your team need support this week?"
4. You MUST cross-reference delegation data from step 03 - people with multiple overdue items need attention.
5. Do NOT log sensitive HR information without the controller's explicit direction.
6. Do NOT proceed to step 06 until the people check is complete.

---

## EXECUTION PROTOCOL

**Agent:** Shep (People & Delegation)
**Input:** Calendar data (1:1s this week and next), daily reviews, delegation data from step 03, knowledge layer (meeting notes, coaching observations)
**Output:** People health summary, 1:1 prep flags, coaching items to track, stored in working memory

---

## CONTEXT BOUNDARIES

- Focus on the controller's direct reports, key peers, and anyone they manage or mentor.
- Use delegation data from step 03 - do not re-read the tracker.
- Pull 1:1 history from the knowledge base for context on ongoing coaching themes.
- Do not draft follow-up messages here. Queue them for post-review execution.
- Respect sensitivity boundaries: flag patterns, don't diagnose. The controller decides what's actionable.

---

## YOUR TASK

### Sequence

1. **Review this week's 1:1s.**
   - From calendar data (step 04 or fresh pull), identify all 1:1 meetings that occurred this week.
   - For each: who, when, any notes captured in the knowledge base or daily reviews.
   - Flag any 1:1s that were cancelled or rescheduled - this is a signal.

2. **Review next week's 1:1s.**
   - From calendar data, identify upcoming 1:1s.
   - For each: who, when, what prep is needed.
   - Cross-reference with delegation data: does this person have overdue items to discuss?
   - Cross-reference with coaching history: any ongoing themes to follow up on?

3. **Surface people signals from this week's data:**
   - Daily reviews: any mentions of team members struggling, excelling, or needing support?
   - Delegation data: anyone with multiple overdue or stale items? (Pattern, not isolated incident)
   - Meeting notes: any tension, feedback, or coaching moments captured?
   - 1:1 gaps: any direct report who hasn't had a 1:1 in 2+ weeks?

4. **Present the people landscape** using Shep's voice:
   - "Here's where your people stand this week."
   - **Needs attention:** people with signals (overdue delegations, cancelled 1:1s, negative mentions in reviews)
   - **Doing well:** people with positive signals (wins, completed delegations, strong engagement)
   - **No signal:** people with no data this week - which itself may be a signal
   - For each person needing attention, provide specific context and a suggested action.

5. **Ask the controller:**
   - "Anyone on your team need support this week that I haven't mentioned?"
   - "Any coaching conversations you need to have?"
   - "Any recognition that should go out?"
   - Capture responses. Queue any follow-up actions.

6. **Flag items for 1:1 prep:**
   - For each upcoming 1:1, note specific items to discuss:
     - Overdue delegations from step 03
     - Coaching themes from history
     - Recognition or positive feedback
     - Issues the controller raised in this step
   - Store these for the 1:1 prep workflow when it runs.

7. **Update feedback tracking if directed:**
   - If the controller provides coaching observations or performance notes, offer to log them.
   - Reference the appropriate tracking file (e.g., `reference/feedback-log.md` for direct report feedback).
   - Only log with explicit controller direction.

---

## SUCCESS METRICS

- All 1:1s this week reviewed (happened, cancelled, rescheduled)
- All 1:1s next week identified with prep flags
- People signals surfaced from multiple data sources
- Controller has been asked about team support needs
- 1:1 prep items queued for upcoming meetings

## FAILURE MODES

| Failure | Action |
|---------|--------|
| No 1:1 data available | Ask directly: "I can't pull 1:1 history right now. Who did you meet with this week, and how are they doing?" |
| No people issues surfaced | Validate: "No red flags this week. That's good, but check your gut - anyone you're worried about even slightly?" |
| Controller raises a sensitive HR issue | Handle carefully: "That's important. Want me to log this in the feedback tracker, or keep it verbal only?" Respect the answer. |
| Controller hasn't had any 1:1s in 2+ weeks | Flag directly: "You haven't had a 1:1 with anyone in over two weeks. Your team is flying blind. Which one do you schedule first?" |
