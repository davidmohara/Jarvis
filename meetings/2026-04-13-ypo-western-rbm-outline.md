## Block 1: Provocative Opening (3 min) — David leads

**Setup:** No slides. Just David at center stage.

**Prompt:** "If you were evaluating your chapter the way you evaluate a company before you invest — the leadership team, the financials, the operating rhythm, the bench strength — would you write the check?"

**Room move:**
- Stand if you'd invest without hesitation
- Stay seated if it depends on the terms
- Raise a hand if you'd pass

**Transition line:** "Most of us run businesses that would never survive on hero energy alone. But that's exactly how a lot of chapters operate. Today we're going to name that gap and leave with one move to close it."

→ Hand to Veronica

---

## Block 2: Table Exercise — Professionalization Spectrum (5 min) — Veronica leads

**Visual on screen:** One slide showing 5 dimensions with a 1–5 scale

| Dimension | 1 = Hero-Driven | 5 = System-Driven |
|-----------|-----------------|-------------------|
| Governance & Role Clarity | "One person runs everything" | "Roles documented, transitions smooth" |
| Financial Discipline | "Chair manages the budget in their head" | "Transparent reporting, reserves policy" |
| Strategic Planning | "We wing it year to year" | "Written 3-year plan, reviewed quarterly" |
| Member Experience Design | "Events depend on who volunteers" | "Programmed calendar, feedback loops" |
| Board Transition & Continuity | "Momentum resets every rotation" | "Onboarding process, institutional memory" |

**Instructions (Veronica):**
- "At your tables, rate your chapter 1–5 on each dimension. Be honest — this isn't a report card, it's a diagnostic."
- "Then identify the ONE dimension where your chapter is most personality-dependent. Where would things break if that person left?"

**Timer:** 4 minutes table work, 1 minute debrief

**Debrief:** Veronica calls on 2 tables: "What surprised you?"

→ Hand to David

---

## Block 3: Teaching Burst — Hero → System Leadership (7 min) — David leads

**Slide: Three Shifts**

| From | To |
|------|----|
| Passion-driven | Process-enabled |
| Reactive | Strategic |
| Individual excellence | Institutional excellence |

**David's delivery notes:**
- Keep this tight — 4 minutes of content, max. This is a framework, not a lecture.
- Use ONE real example. Best candidate: a chapter (or a business) that was thriving under a strong leader, leader rotated out, everything collapsed. The system didn't exist — only the hero did.
- Connect to the room: "How many of you have seen this in a chapter? How many of you have BEEN the hero?"

**Discussion prompt (2 min):** "Turn to someone at your table and answer this: where is your chapter relying on hero energy right now?"

→ Hand to Veronica

---

## Block 4: Scenario Challenge (8 min) — Veronica facilitates, David timekeeps

**Pre-printed table cards** (one scenario per table, doubled if >4 tables):

- **Scenario A:** Your Chapter Chair rotates out and momentum collapses. New chair inherits nothing — no playbook, no budget context, no event calendar.
- **Scenario B:** Events are strong and well-attended, but nobody knows the chapter's financial position. The treasurer reports verbally once a year.
- **Scenario C:** Membership growth is strong but engagement is inconsistent. 30% of members attend regularly; the rest are ghosts.
- **Scenario D:** Board meetings are 90 minutes of status updates. No strategic discussion. No decisions. People stop showing up.

**Instructions (Veronica):**
1. Diagnose the root cause — what's the system failure?
2. Identify the missing professional structure
3. Recommend ONE structural fix that would survive a leadership change

**Timer:** 5 minutes table work → David calls time

**Share-out:** Veronica calls on 2 tables only (not all). "Give us your scenario and your one fix in 30 seconds."

→ Both close together

---

## Block 5: 90-Day Move + Close (7 min) — David & Veronica together

**David reframes the prompt:**
"Don't just pick something to improve. Pick the one thing that would still work if your chapter chair left tomorrow. That's the test of a system."

**Individual + partner work (3 min):**
- Write: "In the next 90 days, we will professionalize ________"
- Share with a partner at the table
- Partner pressure-tests: "Would that survive a leadership transition?"

**Examples on screen** (not read aloud — just visual prompts):
- Formal board onboarding with written playbook
- Written 3-year strategic plan reviewed quarterly
- Annual member experience survey with action plan
- Defined KPIs for engagement tracked monthly
- Financial reporting dashboard shared with full board

**Commitment moment (2 min) — Veronica:**
"Stand up if you're willing to commit to your 90-day move."
Call on 2–3 people: "Tell the room what you're committing to."

**Close — David (30 sec):**
"Great chapters aren't built on energy alone. They're built on systems that outlast any single leader. You've named your gap. You've got 90 days. Go make it real."

## Pacing Risk

35 minutes is tight for five blocks with table exercises. If you're running long, cut the Block 4 share-out from 2 tables to 1, or skip the Block 2 debrief entirely (the table work itself has the value). Never cut Block 5 — the commitment is the whole point.
