**Season 1, Episode 5**

# Orchestrating Agents: The New Scarce Skill

---

## Episode Overview

We'll explore what it actually means to orchestrate multiple AI agents to tackle complex tasks — and why this is quickly becoming one of the most valuable skills in enterprise technology. Robyn brings a practitioner's lens from running a large delivery organization, drawing parallels between leading teams of people and coordinating teams of AI agents.

---

## Key Discussion Topics

| # | TOPIC | FOCUS |
|---|-------|-------|
| 1 | **Agent Orchestration Defined** | What it means to coordinate multiple AI agents on complex tasks — beyond just using a single chatbot. |
| 2 | **The Team Analogy** | How managing a team of AI agents compares to managing a team of people — delegation, context-setting, quality control. |
| 3 | **The Orchestration Layer** | What the coordination framework actually looks like in practice — code, prompts, products, or something else entirely. |
| 4 | **Failure Modes & Oversight** | What happens when agents don't coordinate well, and how to keep humans in the loop as complexity grows. |
| 5 | **The Skills Gap** | Who inside organizations should own this capability, and when enterprises should start thinking multi-agent. |

---

## Prompting Questions

1. **On Defining It**
*"When we say 'orchestrating AI agents,' what does that actually mean? Paint the picture for someone who's only used ChatGPT."*

2. **On The Team Analogy**
*"You run a large delivery org. How does managing a team of AI agents compare to managing a team of people? Where does the analogy hold and where does it break?"*

3. **On The Orchestration Layer**
*"What does the 'orchestration layer' actually look like? Is it code? Prompts? A product? Walk us through it."*

4. **On Failure Modes**
*"What happens when agents don't coordinate well? And how do you keep humans in the loop when you have multiple agents running?"*

5. **On The Skills Gap**
*"This skill didn't exist two years ago. Who in the org should own it — developers, leaders, both? And when should enterprises start thinking about multi-agent orchestration?"*

6. **Wrap-Up**
*"What are a couple of takeaways you'd like the listeners to walk away with?"*
