## David's Talking Points & Angles

- **Improving as a case study:** Talk about how Improving itself is architecting AI into its business — the workshops, the agent systems, the consulting model. You're not just advising clients, you're doing it internally.
- **Architecture isn't just technical:** The biggest mistake companies make is treating AI as a tech project. It's a business model decision. The architecture includes people, process, and governance — not just APIs and data pipelines.
- **Data is the moat:** Most companies aren't AI-limited, they're data-limited. The ones who got their data strategy right 5 years ago are the ones winning with AI now.
- **John's builder perspective:** Draw out John's experience building real systems. He's not a strategist talking theory — he's a developer who's wired enterprise systems together. Get him to describe what it actually looks like under the hood.
- **The governance trap:** Companies that move fast without governance will move backwards even faster when something breaks. Give a real example.
- **Start with one use case:** Practical advice — don't try to boil the ocean. Pick one high-value process, prove it, then expand. The architecture should enable this iterative approach.
