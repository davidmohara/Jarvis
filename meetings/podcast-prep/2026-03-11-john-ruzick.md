## Episode Topic

**"Architecting the AI-Driven Business Model"**

This episode goes beyond the "AI is transforming everything" surface to dig into what it actually takes to build an organization's technology, data, and culture to be AI-ready. Not a strategy talk — an architecture talk. What are the non-negotiable components, where do companies get stuck, and what does the future look like?

Core themes:
- What an AI-driven business model actually looks like beyond automation
- How AI changes value creation and delivery
- Data and governance strategy as non-negotiables
- Human-AI collaboration — using AI as a tool, not a replacement
- Challenges: ethical considerations, organizational resistance, cultural shift
- Opportunities: generative AI trends, customer experience, new business models
- Skills a business needs to succeed in an AI-first world

## David's Talking Points & Angles

- **Personal experience — Jarvis as a case study:** You've built a multi-agent AI system (Jarvis) that runs your executive operations — daily briefings, inbox triage, pipeline reviews, 1:1 prep, even this podcast prep document. That's not theory. That's architecture in action. Use it to ground the conversation: "Here's what I built, here's what broke, here's what I learned."

- **Improving as the lab:** Improving isn't just advising clients on AI — you're doing it internally. The AI workshops, the agent systems, the consulting methodology. Talk about what Improving looks like as a company that's architecting AI into its own operations, not just selling it to others.

- **Data is the moat:** Most companies aren't AI-limited, they're data-limited. The ones who got their data strategy right 5 years ago are the ones winning with AI now. John's background in SQL, Hadoop, and data infrastructure makes this a natural thread — draw him out on what "data-ready" actually means.

- **Architecture isn't just technical:** The biggest mistake companies make is treating AI as a tech project. It's a business model decision. The architecture includes people, process, and governance — not just APIs and data pipelines. Push John on this — as a Technical Director, where does his job stop being technical and start being organizational?

- **John's builder perspective:** He's not a strategist talking theory — he's a developer who's wired enterprise systems together for 12+ years. Get him to describe what it actually looks like under the hood when you're re-architecting for AI. What breaks? What surprises you?

- **The governance trap:** Companies that move fast without governance will move backwards even faster when something breaks. John's experience with data governance (SSAS, SSRS, enterprise reporting) gives him a real perspective here.

- **Start with one use case:** Practical advice — don't try to boil the ocean. Pick one high-value process, prove it, then expand. The architecture should enable this iterative approach. This is also how Improving advises clients.

- **Contrarian take:** Is "AI-driven business model" even the right framing? Maybe the better frame is "data-driven business model with AI acceleration." Challenge the premise a bit — it makes for better conversation.
