## Suggested Questions (if no Janine doc arrives)

1. **Define it simply.** "When we say 'orchestrating AI agents,' what does that actually mean? Paint the picture for someone who's only used ChatGPT."

2. **Why now?** "Six months ago nobody was talking about agent orchestration. What changed? Why is this suddenly the skill everyone needs?"

8. **Human oversight.** "How do you keep humans in the loop when you have multiple agents running? At what point does it become unmanageable?"

9. **Build vs. buy.** "Should companies build their own orchestration frameworks or wait for platforms to mature?"

10. **What's next.** "Where does agent orchestration go in the next 12-18 months? What should leaders be watching?"

## David's Talking Points & Angles

- **Personal experience:** You literally built a multi-agent system (Jarvis). Talk about what you learned orchestrating Chief, Chase, Shep, Harper, Quinn — each with distinct roles, handoff protocols, and failure modes. This is not theoretical for you.
- **The "conductor" metaphor:** The skill isn't AI engineering — it's more like conducting an orchestra. You need to know what each instrument does, when it should play, and how to keep them in sync.
- **Scarce skill = scarce value:** Frame this as a career opportunity. The people who learn this now will be the most valuable people in enterprise AI in 2-3 years.
- **Governance matters:** Multi-agent systems compound risk. Each agent can hallucinate, and when they hand off to each other, errors multiply. Governance isn't optional — it's the difference between a demo and a production system.
- **Improving's angle:** Position Improving as already building this capability for clients — this isn't future talk, it's happening now.
