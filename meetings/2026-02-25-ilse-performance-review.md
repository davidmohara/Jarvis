## Conversation Arc (25 min)

Lead with transparency. She saw the email from Ope asking for feedback — she knows it's coming. Get ahead of it.

> "Ilse, you saw the email from Ope checking in on your performance. I'm going to be sharing feedback with him, and I wanted to make sure you hear it from me first. No surprises, no misunderstandings — that's how I operate. I want to use this time to walk through where things stand."

> "The daily work — calendar, travel, expenses — you've been solid on that, and I appreciate it. But I owe you honesty on where the gaps are too."

### 2. What's Working (3 min)

Acknowledge these specifically — she needs to hear them:

- **YPO reimbursement follow-through.** Persisted when Jennifer Jensen's predecessor lost the request. Got it to accounting. That's the kind of tenacity I need.
- **Rob/Confluent meeting.** Handled the last-minute time change, provided the office address, adapted. Clean execution.
- **DCEO Summit photos.** Well-organized, properly named, filed same day.
- **Calendar volume.** Managing a high-tempo schedule across multiple workstreams without dropping active items.

### 3. Set Context (2 min)

Before getting into specifics, name the expectation gap clearly:

> "When Wing brought you on, you were presented to me as a Senior EA. That set a certain bar — someone who anticipates needs, owns systems, and operates as a strategic partner. I want to be transparent: what I'm seeing right now is closer to entry-level execution. The daily tasks get done, and I appreciate that. But the senior-level work — the proactive stuff, the system ownership — isn't there yet."

*This isn't an attack on Ilse — it's naming that the gap between what was sold and what's being delivered is real. The accountability for that positioning sits with Ope, and you'll address it there. With Ilse, the point is: here's the bar, here's where you are, let's close it.*

### 4. The Gap (10 min)

Frame it as the **difference between executing tasks and owning the operation.**

**Lead with the question, not the verdict:**

> "When you think about your role, do you see yourself as someone who completes tasks I assign — or as someone who runs my operation and tells me what needs to happen next?"

Let her answer. Then walk through the three themes:

**Theme 1: The Nasdaq bio misdirect**

> "The bio you sent to Teresa at UTB ended up at Nasdaq Governance Solutions. They replied saying it was sent by mistake. That kind of thing reflects on me. Mistakes happen — I'm not looking for you to add more steps or slow down. But when something like that lands, I need you to catch it and clean it up without me having to find it."

*The point isn't "add a verification checklist" — it's own the cleanup. If something goes wrong, fix it. Don't wait for David to discover it.*

**Theme 2: Systems aren't being maintained**

> "I went through Our Share and the Playbooks folder. There are two separate speaking opportunity trackers with completely different data in each. The event tracking spreadsheet you created in December is still empty. The Corporate Relations playbook is still raw notes. The SOPs you built during onboarding haven't been touched in three months."

*Don't pile on — pick 2-3 specifics and let the pattern speak for itself.*

> "I need those systems to be alive, not artifacts. If a tracker exists, it should be current. If it's not useful, kill it. But don't leave it half-built."

**Theme 3: Proactive vs. reactive**

> "Almost everything in our workflow starts with me telling you what to do. I need you to start getting ahead of me. If you see a calendar conflict with something I committed to, flag it before I ask. If a reimbursement is overdue, chase it without me prompting. If someone emails me requesting a meeting, propose times instead of asking me what to do."

*Reference the OmniFocus task she doesn't know about: "Give Ilse feedback — be more proactive." This has been on your mind.*

### 5. Set the Bar (5 min)

Give her 3 concrete things to own immediately:

1. **Consolidate the speaking trackers into one file by end of this week.** Kill the duplicate or merge them. One source of truth.
2. **Weekly proactive briefing aligned to our Friday meeting.** Before we meet, send what's on my plate for the next week, what's still pending, and what she sees coming. Even if it's 3 bullets in Teams — come prepared, not waiting for direction.
3. **Own the cleanup.** When something goes sideways — wrong recipient, stale file, missed detail — catch it and fix it before I find it. I don't need more process. I need fewer surprises.

### 6. Close with Investment (2 min)

> "I'm sharing this because I want you to succeed in this role. The daily stuff is solid. I need you to level up on the system side — own it, maintain it, get ahead of it. That's the difference between good and great."

Ask: "What do you need from me to make that happen?"

*Let her respond. Note anything she flags as a blocker — tools, access, clarity, time.*
