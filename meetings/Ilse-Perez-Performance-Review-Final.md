## Areas for Growth

I'm sharing these because I believe Ilse is capable of more, and I want to give her a clear picture of what the next level looks like.

**Systems ownership.** Ilse built structure early - trackers, playbooks, SOPs - and I appreciated that initiative. The opportunity now is maintaining and improving what she's built. For example, the speaking opportunity tracker ended up in two separate files with different data in each. The event tracking spreadsheet created in December hasn't been populated yet. The Corporate Relations playbook is still in draft form. The instinct to build systems is a strength. The next step is keeping them alive and current - and if something isn't serving a purpose, consolidating or retiring it so the information stays clean.

**Proactive ownership.** Ilse is strong at executing what's asked of her. The growth opportunity is moving toward anticipating what's needed before being asked. For example, flagging a calendar conflict before I discover it, chasing an overdue reimbursement without a prompt, or proposing meeting times when a request comes in rather than waiting for direction. This is the shift from good to great in the EA role, and I think she has the foundation to make it.

**Attention to detail on outbound work.** There was an instance where a document was sent to the wrong recipient - a mix-up between two contacts. These things happen. The coaching opportunity here is building a habit of verifying recipients and giving outbound communications a final review before they go out on my behalf. I've also noticed some small typos across documents that a quick second pass would catch. Anything external-facing represents me and the company, so it needs to be polished.
