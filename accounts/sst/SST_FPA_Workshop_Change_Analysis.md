### 5.4 — Slides 33–36: Construction Industry Stage Reality (REPLACE ENTIRELY)

**Location:** Slides 33, 34, 35, 36

**Current content:** Four slides covering the construction industry — AI maturity stage reality, hard data stats, what's deployed vs. not, and stage-by-stage breakdown. All content is construction-specific (Procore, Autodesk, Bluebeam, ALICE Technologies, Bechtel, etc.).

**Action:** Replace all four slides with FP&A / Finance industry equivalents. Suggested structure:

**Slide 33 replacement — Finance/FP&A Industry Positioning:**
> Finance & FP&A
> Majority at Stage 2–3 | Leaders at Stage 4 | Ceiling: Stage 5

**Slide 34 replacement — Finance: The Hard Data:**
> - 80%+ of FP&A teams still rely on Excel as their primary planning tool (Gartner 2024)
> - 74% of CFOs cite data quality and timeliness as top AI adoption barriers (Deloitte 2025)
> - AI-assisted close processes reduce cycle time by 30–50% at leading adopters (BlackLine 2025)
> - Only 12% of finance functions have deployed AI beyond basic automation (McKinsey 2025)
>
> The function is data-rich but execution-poor: structured financial data is ideal for AI, but fragmented systems, manual handoffs, and governance concerns keep most teams at Stage 2.

**Slide 35 replacement — Finance: What's Deployed vs. What's Not:**

*Deployed Today:*
> - Microsoft Copilot for Finance — automated variance commentary, Excel integration
> - BlackLine AI — anomaly detection in journal entries, reconciliation automation
> - Anaplan / Adaptive — AI-assisted rolling forecasts and scenario modeling
> - Workday Illuminate — natural language querying of financial data
> - SAP Joule — conversational AI across FI/CO modules
> - Power BI Copilot — AI-generated narrative summaries of financial dashboards

*Not Happening Yet:*
> - Autonomous close processes without human sign-off at any major company
> - Multi-agent FP&A orchestration (forecast → variance → commentary → distribution)
> - AI systems with authority to adjust budget allocations without CFO approval
> - Fully autonomous audit response or regulatory filing
> - Agentic treasury management at enterprise scale

**Slide 36 replacement — Finance: Stage-by-Stage Reality:**
> Stage 1–2: MAJORITY HERE — Excel-based planning, Copilot for drafting commentary, basic Power BI. Human approves everything. 80%+ of teams.
>
> Stage 3: ACTIVE (LEADERS) — BlackLine anomaly detection auto-flagging exceptions. AI-generated first-draft variance commentary. Workday/SAP natural language queries without human approval for reads.
>
> Stage 4: EMERGING — Anaplan AI suggesting forecast adjustments; human reviews and accepts. Automated reconciliation with exception-only human review. Some teams approaching autonomous month-end checklists.
>
> Stage 5–8: NOT YET — No FP&A team runs autonomous multi-agent close, forecast, and reporting cycles. Governance and data quality gaps are the primary blockers. Near-possible ceiling: Stage 4–5 by late 2026.

### 5.6 — Slide 41: Why the Gap? Accelerators vs. Constraints

**Location:** Slide 41

**Current content:** Compares financial services vs. construction/manufacturing in terms of AI maturity accelerators and constraints. References JPMorgan's $18B tech budget, fintechs, construction workers using paper.

**Action:** This slide's framing is relevant but the examples need to shift. SST's FP&A team should be compared to finance-sector peers, not construction GCs.

**Suggested replacement framing:**

*What Accelerates FP&A AI Maturity:*
> - Structured data by nature — financial transactions are already machine-readable
> - Clear ROI — every hour saved in close cycle or forecast accuracy is measurable
> - Tool ecosystem maturity — SAP, Oracle, Anaplan, BlackLine all have native AI
> - Executive pressure — CFOs under board-level pressure to do more with lean teams

*What Constrains FP&A AI Maturity:*
> - Data fragmentation — ERP, spreadsheets, and shadow systems not integrated
> - Governance requirements — audit trails and SOX compliance slow autonomous adoption
> - Excel dependency — deeply embedded workflows resist system change
> - Talent gap — FP&A teams built for analysis, not AI prompt design or governance
