# Milan — June 5–7, 2026

---

## Friday, June 5 — Arrive & Conquer the Highlights

**Morning / Early Afternoon — Viator Walking Tour (~3 hrs)**
Book the [Essential Milan Tour with Last Supper](https://www.viator.com/tours/Milan/The-Best-of-Milan-Walking-Tour-with-Skip-the-Line-Last-Supper-Tickets/d512-6404P12) or the higher-reviewed [Must See Half-Day Tour](https://www.viator.com/tours/Milan/Milan-Half-Day-Sightseeing-Tour-with-da-Vincis-The-Last-Supper/d512-2872ZV19) (4,300+ reviews, includes La Scala interior). Either one covers:

- **Duomo di Milano** — Gothic cathedral, 500+ years in the making. Rooftop terraces included on some tours; if not, buy the upgrade (€14) for the elevator to the top. Worth it.
- **Galleria Vittorio Emanuele II** — Glass-roofed 19th-century arcade connecting the Duomo to La Scala. Spin your heel on the bull mosaic for good luck.
- **Teatro alla Scala (La Scala)** — The opera house. The half-day tour goes inside the theater and museum.
- **Sforza Castle** — Medieval fortress, Michelangelo's final sculpture (*Rondanini Pietà*) is here.
- **The Last Supper** — Skip-the-line into Santa Maria delle Grazie. 15-minute viewing window. Leonardo painted it directly on the wall — it's not a canvas, it's the room itself.

> ⚠️ **Booking note:** Provide all passenger names at time of booking (required for Last Supper entry). Book by mid-April — these fill up 55+ days out.

**Late Afternoon — Pinacoteca Ambrosiana** *(optional if energy permits)*
10-minute walk from the Duomo. Caravaggio's *Basket of Fruit*, Raphael's cartoon for *School of Athens*, Leonardo's Atlantic Codex. Small museum, 60–90 minutes.

**Evening — Aperitivo in Brera**
Milan's signature ritual, 6–8 PM. Order a Negroni or Aperol Spritz at any bar in the Brera district and it comes with a spread of food — meats, cheeses, bruschetta, olives. It's not happy hour, it's a lifestyle. Try **Bar Basso** (invented the Negroni Sbagliato) or any spot along Via Fiori Chiari.

**Dinner — Cotoletta & Risotto Night**
First proper Milanese dinner. Two dishes you must have this trip:

- **Risotto alla Milanese** — saffron risotto, golden and creamy
- **Cotoletta alla Milanese** — the original breaded veal cutlet (bone-in, not chicken)

Look for a traditional trattoria — **Trattoria Milanese** (open since 1933) or **Ratanà** near Porta Nuova for a more modern take.

## Saturday, June 6 — Food Tour + Deep Art

**Morning — Food Walking Tour (~3 hrs)**
Two strong options:

- [**Brera Small-Group Food Tour**](https://www.viator.com/tours/Milan/Milan-Food-Walking-Tour-of-Brera/d512-3092MILANFOODIES) — 100-year-old family shops, Parma ham, panzerotti, wine bars. Stays in the charming Brera streets.
- [**Navigli District Food Tour**](https://www.viator.com/Milan-tours/Food-Tours/d512-g6-c80) — Canal-side, more casual vibe. Sicilian, Neapolitan, and Milanese street food. Great for a warm June morning.

> I'd lean Brera if you want history and atmosphere, Navigli if you want the canal photos and a more relaxed pace.

**Afternoon — Pinacoteca di Brera**
Milan's premier art gallery. Raphael's *Marriage of the Virgin*, Caravaggio's *Supper at Emmaus*, Bellini, Mantegna's stunning *Lamentation of Christ* (the foreshortening is jaw-dropping). Allow 2 hours.

**Alternative / Add-on: Fondazione Prada**
If contemporary art and architecture are calling, this Rem Koolhaas–designed complex is visually wild — a gold-leafed haunted house, a cinema, rotating exhibitions. Bar Luce inside was designed by Wes Anderson. About 20 minutes south by taxi.

**Evening — Navigli Canals**
If you did the Brera food tour in the morning, head to the Navigli district for the evening. The canals come alive at sunset — bars, street artists, antique shops. Great for a slow walk before dinner.

**Dinner — Ossobuco Night**
Complete the Milanese trinity: **ossobuco** (braised veal shank with gremolata), ideally served with risotto alla milanese. **Antica Trattoria della Pesa** is a classic choice, or **Al Pont de Ferr** on the Navigli canal for ambiance.

## Sunday, June 7 — Morning Gems & Departure (1:45 PM flight)

*Allow 90 minutes to get to Malpensa (MXP) or 45 minutes for Linate (LIN). Aim to leave the city center by 10:30 AM for Malpensa, 11:15 AM for Linate.*

**8:00 AM — Coffee at a Historic Café**
Start at **Cova** (1817) or **Biffi** (1867) in the Galleria. Cappuccino and brioche — the Milanese breakfast ritual. Both are old-world institutions worth sitting in.

**8:45 AM — San Sepolcro Underground Crypt**
Built on the ruins of the ancient Roman forum beneath the San Sepolcro church. Millennia-old white cobblestones and medieval frescoes underground. Not on most tourist maps — quick, powerful, 30–45 minutes. Just a few minutes' walk from the Galleria.

**9:30 AM — Duomo Rooftop** *(if not covered on Friday's tour)*
If the walking tour didn't include rooftop access, this is worth circling back for. The Gothic spires up close, the marble detail, and the city panorama are the kind of thing you remember. Skip this if you're short on time.

**10:15–10:30 AM — Head to airport**
