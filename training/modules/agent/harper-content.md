## What You'll Learn

How to plan and track thought leadership output using Harper — building a cadence for content that advances your professional brand and your organization's positioning.

## Before We Start

Complete Email Drafting. The user should be comfortable with Harper's voice calibration. They should have a sense of what they want to be known for professionally.

## Walkthrough

### Step 1: Define Your Platform (5 min)

Trigger the `harper-content` skill or say "help me plan my content."

Harper asks:
- What topics are you the authority on?
- Where do you publish? (LinkedIn, blog, podcast, internal comms)
- How often can you realistically produce content? (Weekly, biweekly, monthly)
- What's the purpose? (Thought leadership, recruiting, sales enablement, internal culture)

**Coaching prompt:** "Consistency beats quality in content. One decent post per week beats one brilliant post per quarter. The algorithm and your audience reward showing up regularly."

### Step 2: Build the Calendar (7 min)

Harper generates a 2-week content plan:

For each entry:
- Date
- Topic (connected to their authority areas)
- Channel (where it publishes)
- Format (post, article, video script, podcast talking points)
- Hook (opening line or concept)

**Coaching prompt:** "The hook is everything. If the first line doesn't stop the scroll, nothing else matters. Harper writes hooks that earn attention — but only if the topic is real. Don't post about things you don't actually care about."

### Step 3: Create One Piece (5 min)

Pick one entry from the calendar and draft it right now:

- Harper generates the first draft
- User reviews and adjusts voice
- Finalize for publishing

**Coaching prompt:** "The hardest part of content is starting. We just eliminated that. You now have a draft in hand. Polish it, post it, move on."

### Step 4: Tracking and Rhythm (3 min)

Show how the content calendar tracks:
- Published vs. planned
- Topics covered (avoiding repetition)
- Engagement if data is available

Set up the cadence: "Every [day], Harper will prompt you with your next content topic and a draft hook."

## Reflection

Ask: "What's the one message you want your audience to associate with your name?"

Everything in the content calendar should ladder to that.

## Success Criteria

Plan and track thought leadership output for 2 weeks. Create at least one piece of content during the module.
