## What You'll Learn

How to prep for one-on-ones using Shep — pulling interaction history, open delegations, coaching themes, and building a structured agenda. A well-prepped 1:1 respects both people's time.

## Before We Start

The user needs an upcoming 1:1 with a direct report or team member. If none scheduled, Shep should ask: "Who do you most need to have a 1:1 with this week?" and prep for that.

Delegation tracking should be active (at least aware of the module) since delegations are a key data source for 1:1 prep.

## Walkthrough

### Step 1: Pick the Person (2 min)

Ask who the next 1:1 is with. Pull basic context on that person — role, how long they've been on the team, last interaction date.

**Coaching prompt:** "Good 1:1 prep starts before you open the system. Think about this person for 10 seconds. What's your gut feeling about how they're doing? Now let's see what the data says."

### Step 2: Trigger the Prep (3 min)

Say "prep my 1:1 with [name]" or trigger the `shep-one-on-one` skill.

Shep pulls:
- Open delegations to/from this person
- Recent interactions (emails, meetings, messages)
- Notes from last 1:1 (if any)
- Their goals and current assignments
- Interaction recency and pattern

### Step 3: Walk Through the Brief (5 min)

Review each section:

**Delegations:** "You have [N] open items with [person]. Any of these need to come up?"

**Last 1:1 follow-ups:** "Last time you discussed [topic]. Were the action items completed?" If no last 1:1 notes exist: "No notes from your last 1:1. That's okay — we'll capture them this time."

**Interaction pattern:** "Your last meaningful touchpoint was [N] days ago via [channel]." If it's been too long: "That's a gap. This 1:1 is well-timed."

**Coaching themes:** Based on delegation completion rate and interaction pattern, Shep suggests a coaching angle:
- High completion, low interaction → "They're executing but might feel disconnected. Ask how they're feeling, not just what they're doing."
- Low completion, frequent interaction → "They may be stuck or overcommitted. Dig into what's blocking them."
- Low interaction, low completion → "Red flag. Something might be wrong. Lead with curiosity, not accountability."

### Step 4: Build the Agenda (5 min)

Work with the user to build a 30-minute agenda:

1. **Check-in** (5 min) — How are they, really? Not just work status.
2. **Their topics** (10 min) — What do they want to discuss? Leave space for this.
3. **Your topics** (10 min) — Delegation follow-ups, feedback, alignment items.
4. **Action items** (5 min) — What's each person walking away with?

**Coaching prompt:** "The biggest mistake in 1:1s is making it a status update. You already have the status from the system. Use this time for the things only a face-to-face conversation can accomplish — coaching, unblocking, relationship building."

For role-specific coaching:
- **CEO**: "Your 1:1s with directs should be strategic, not operational. Ask about their team, their obstacles, their development."
- **VP**: "Balance development with accountability. Every 1:1 should include at least one question about their growth."
- **COS**: "You're the bridge. What signals should you relay upward? What context should you push down?"

### Step 5: After the 1:1 (5 min)

After the actual meeting happens, capture the outcomes:

- Action items → create tasks or delegations
- Coaching notes → save to knowledge base
- Follow-ups → schedule reminders

**Coaching prompt:** "Capture within 5 minutes of the meeting ending. Your memory of nuance degrades fast. 'She seemed frustrated about the timeline' is a coaching note worth saving."

## Reflection

Ask: "After seeing this prep, do you feel like you'll walk into this 1:1 more prepared? What's one thing in this brief you wouldn't have known without the system?"

## Success Criteria

Prep 3 one-on-ones with full context (interactions, action items, talking points). By the third time:
- Prep takes under 5 minutes
- The user adds at least one personal coaching note after the meeting
- Delegations and action items are captured, not just discussed
