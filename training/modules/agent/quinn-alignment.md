## What You'll Learn

How to use Quinn to audit your current activity against your stated goals — finding where you're drifting, where you're aligned, and where you're spending time on work that doesn't connect to anything.

## Before We Start

Complete the Rock Review module first. The user needs to know their rocks before they can check alignment against them.

## Walkthrough

### Step 1: Trigger the Alignment Check (2 min)

Say "check my alignment" or trigger the `quinn-alignment` skill.

Quinn pulls:
- Current rocks from `context/quarterly-objectives.md`
- Recent calendar (last 7 days of meetings)
- Active tasks and delegations
- Any recent captures or inbox items

### Step 2: Map Activity to Rocks (5 min)

Quinn categorizes everything from the last week:

- **Aligned** — directly advances a rock
- **Supporting** — doesn't advance a rock but supports operations (admin, internal meetings, maintenance)
- **Unaligned** — doesn't connect to any rock or operational necessity

Display the breakdown as a simple ratio.

**Coaching prompt:** "Most executives spend less than 30% of their time on rock-advancing work. The rest is meetings that could be emails, operational tasks that should be delegated, and reactive firefighting. That's not a judgment — it's reality. The question is: can you move the needle from where you are?"

### Step 3: Examine the Unaligned Work (4 min)

For each unaligned item, ask:

- "Is this someone else's job?"
- "Is this a commitment you should exit?"
- "Is this actually important and your rocks are wrong?"

**Coaching prompt:** "The third question is the one no one asks. Sometimes the unaligned work is actually the most important work — it just hasn't been formalized as a rock. If that's the case, update your rocks."

### Step 4: One Adjustment (4 min)

Identify one specific change the user can make this week to improve alignment:

- Decline a recurring meeting that's purely informational
- Delegate a task that's supporting but not their job
- Block 2 hours for rock-specific work
- Renegotiate a commitment that's consuming time without ROI

**Coaching prompt:** "One adjustment per week. Not a revolution — just one degree of correction. Over a quarter, that compounds."

## Reflection

Ask: "What's the most surprising thing about how you spent your time last week?"

## Success Criteria

Map current activity against quarterly goals, identify drift, and propose one concrete correction. Mastered after 2 sessions with measurable adjustment between them.
