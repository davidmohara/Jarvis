## What You'll Learn

How to run a pipeline review with Chase — assess deal health, identify at-risk opportunities, and determine next actions for moving deals forward.

## Before We Start

The user needs active deals or opportunities to review. If CRM is connected, Chase pulls from there. If not, the user can describe their current pipeline and Chase builds a working model.

## Walkthrough

### Step 1: Trigger the Review (3 min)

Say "review my pipeline" or trigger the `chase-pipeline` skill.

Chase pulls:
- Active deals/opportunities with stage, value, close date
- Recent activity on each (meetings, emails, proposals sent)
- Deal age and velocity

**Coaching prompt:** "Pipeline reviews aren't about the numbers — they're about the stories behind the numbers. Every deal has a narrative. Chase helps you see the whole picture."

### Step 2: Health Check (5 min)

For each deal, Chase assesses:

- **Stage accuracy** — Is this deal really where you say it is?
- **Activity level** — When was the last meaningful interaction?
- **Timeline** — Is the close date realistic given the stage?
- **Risk flags** — No activity in 14+ days, no champion identified, decision maker not engaged

Walk through the top 3-5 deals:

**Coaching prompt:** "The most dangerous deals are the ones in 'Proposal Sent' that haven't moved in 3 weeks. They feel active because you did work. But the customer has gone quiet. That's not a pipeline — that's a wish list."

### Step 3: Prioritize Actions (5 min)

For each at-risk or high-value deal, identify the one next action:

- Stalled deal → "Who's the champion? When did you last talk to them? That's your next call."
- Closing soon → "What could still kill this? Identify the risk and address it proactively."
- Early stage → "Is the pain real and funded? Don't invest time until you know."

### Step 4: Portfolio View (5 min)

Zoom out to the whole pipeline:

- Total pipeline value vs. quota/target
- Stage distribution (healthy pipeline has a funnel shape, not a bulge in one stage)
- Average deal cycle time
- Win rate trends

**Coaching prompt:** "If 80% of your pipeline is in 'Discovery,' you have a prospecting pipeline, not a closing pipeline. If 80% is in 'Negotiation,' you'll either have a great quarter or a terrible one."

### Step 5: Update and Plan (2 min)

Update any deal stages or notes that changed during the review. Capture action items for specific deals.

## Reflection

Ask: "Which deal in your pipeline are you most confident about? Which one should you probably kill?"

The willingness to disqualify bad deals is a sign of pipeline maturity.

## Success Criteria

Run 2 pipeline reviews, identify at-risk deals, propose next actions for at least 3 deals.
